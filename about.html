Effective Ways to Reduce Food Wastage

  • Plan Your Meals: Prepare a weekly meal plan before shopping so you buy only what you need.
  • Store Food Properly: Keep fruits, vegetables, and other items in the right storage conditions to keep them fresh longer.
  • Control Portions: Serve smaller portions first and take more only if needed to avoid leftovers.
  • Reuse Leftovers: Use leftover food to prepare new meals such as soups, salads, or fried rice.
  • Donate Extra Food: Share surplus food with NGOs or community kitchens to help people in need.

Food wastage is a serious global issue. Large quantities of edible food are thrown away every day by restaurants, hotels, events, and households. At the same time, many people around the world struggle to get enough food.

The Food Wastage Reduction System is designed to reduce this gap. It connects food donors with NGOs and volunteers who can distribute food to people who are in need.

Through this platform, donors can easily share details of surplus food. They can provide information such as food name, quantity, location, and the available pickup time.

NGOs and volunteers can access the system to view available donations and arrange food collection and distribution efficiently.

This system helps reduce food waste while supporting communities. It encourages responsible food management and promotes a more sustainable and caring society.
